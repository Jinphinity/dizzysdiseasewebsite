# Research: Biopunk, Biocomputing & Neural Viruses

Dizzy's Disease is grounded in the "Biopunk" subgenre, specifically the intersection of biological matter and computational networking.

## 1. Wetware CPUs (The "Disease" Core)
The concept of using living brain tissue (neurons/organoids) as processing units.
- **The Horror:** The computer isn't just a machine; it's a *someone*.
- **The Virus:** "Dizzy's Disease" is the failure of the biological interface. The neurons over-fire, creating the "Dizzy" effect (a visual and neurological feedback loop).
- **Web Influence:** The website can mimic "Biological Data Corruption" — text that pulses, shifts color, or bleeds beyond its container.

## 2. Neural Viruses & Snow Crash Influence
A virus that transmits via sensory input (vision/sound) rather than fluid.
- **Visual Strobe:** High-frequency kaleidoscopic patterns that "brute force" the human visual cortex.
- **The ARG Angle:** The "Pattern" is hidden in marketing materials. Once "seen," the user's profile on the website starts tracking "Neural Corruption."

## 3. Organic Intelligence (OI) vs Artificial Intelligence (AI)
The game world relies on "Designer Organisms" grown for specific purposes (Surgical Harvesting, Barricade growth).
- **Harvesting:** Players aren't just looting metal parts; they are "harvesting" biological components like nacre, bone-grafts, or neural-filaments.
- **Website Mechanic:** A "Surgical Hub" where the user clicks "Harvest" on a biological asset image, requiring a steady-hand (wobbly aim) mini-game to get a "High Quality" yield.

## 4. "And I Must Scream" (The Philosophical Horror)
Zombies in Dizzy's Disease aren't just mindless; they are "Desynchronized." The biological "wetware" is still conscious but has lost motor control to the virus.
- **User Engagement:** Finding "Audio Logs" on the website that are just the computer's biological fans (breathing) or data entries that sound like pleas for help.
