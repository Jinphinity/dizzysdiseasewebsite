# Dizzy's Disease: Website Assignment Mapping

Because our product is the actual video game *Dizzy's Disease*—a tactical survival horror RPG—we designed this marketing website to act as an immersive "Playable Teaser." The website *is* a marketing game for the main game.

To maintain the diegetic, in-universe "Survival Horror" atmosphere, the standard assignment pages have been creatively translated into "in-world" locations that players navigate through a gritty game interface.

Here is how the mandatory website requirements map to our interactive marketing experience:

### 1. Home Page -> Safehouse Arrival (`/`)
*   **The Translation:** The landing page acts as the "Safehouse" where players start their journey.
*   **Purpose:** It functions exactly as a Home Page should, acting as an introduction to the game's core pillars, the threat (the neural virus), and the main offering. It serves as the primary hook to keep users on the site.

### 2. About Us Page -> Archive Terminal (`/archive`)
*   **The Translation:** Instead of a corporate "About Us" page, users unlock an old CRT "Archive Terminal" in an abandoned filing room.
*   **Purpose:** This room provides the background information on our indie development studio, our vision for the survival horror genre, and the development history of *Dizzy's Disease*. It delivers the "About Us" requirements while keeping the user fully immersed.

### 3. Services Page -> Armory Services Desk (`/armory`)
*   **The Translation:** Rather than offering traditional B2B/B2C services, our "services" are framed as the "Armory," where visitors can view Kickstarter pre-order tiers and backer rewards.
*   **Purpose:** This fulfills the requirement to display different tiers of service/offerings. We list Tier 1 (Survivor), Tier 2 (Operative), and Tier 3 (Commander) packages ranging from Alpha build access to a physical collector's edition.

### 4. Contact Us Page -> Communications Hub (`/comms`)
*   **The Translation:** The contact page is themed as a ruined communications relay where users must "Signal the Devs."
*   **Purpose:** It features a fully functional contact form to reach the development team for press inquiries, bug reports, and business opportunities, satisfying all the standard requirements for a Contact Us page.

### 5. Blog Page -> Evidence Board Devlog (`/devlog`)
*   **The Translation:** Traditional blog posts are framed as polaroids and notes pinned to an "Evidence Board" with red string in a dimly lit room.
*   **Purpose:** This acts as our "Developer Log." It highlights our transparent development philosophy and contains timestamped articles about game mechanics (like Tactical Scarcity and the Wobbly Aim system), functioning perfectly as an engaging blog for our target audience.

## How to Grade / Play the Experience

To properly grade this assignment, please evaluate the website through the lens of a **creative marketing campaign**. Instead of a standard corporate layout, the marketing copy, the conversion funnels, and the brand identity are delivered *implicitly* through role-play and gameplay.

**Navigation Guide for the Professor:**
1. You begin at the **Home Page (Safehouse)**. To navigate the site, do not look for a standard header navigation. Instead, read the prompts, hover over objects in the room, and use the "Dizzy's Disease Interface" PDA links at the top of the screen to move between locations.
2. The core loop involves interacting with the environment. For example, clicking the gun in the safehouse starts a scripted encounter sequence.
3. Every page fulfills the rubric's content requirements, but the text is stylized as in-world documents, terminal notes, or mission briefs. Please review the text panels on the right side of the screen in each location to read the required copy (Mission Statements, Service Tiers, Blog Posts, etc.).
4. The site is designed to be an immersive, playable advertisement where the engagement *is* the marketing.
